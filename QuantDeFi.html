Cryptocurrency Trading

EconoPhysics-based Cryptocurrency Trading Bot

Overview

Using quantitative approaches to decentralized finance is proving to be the best performing solution: PWC 3rd Annual Global Crypto Hedge Fund Report 2021. MoD's trading algorithm constantly monitors dozens of coins with a frequency of more than one Hertz, so to make autonomous spot trading decisions (long-only positions) 24/7. The ability to scale the quantity of monitored coins allows to manage even large volumes. Customers' portfolios are managed through their Application Programming Interface, which allows to send trade signals without directly managing the funds: the customer always has full visibility of their wallet. The algorithm is compatible with all major international exchanges. Trading strategies are studied also through backtesting, but are made available to the client only after a dry run period.

The bot has been active since the first half of July 2021, excluding most of October 2021.

Principles

The key principles underlying the trading bot range from classical technical analysis to dynamical systems and complex systems theory. Some examples are State Estimation, such as Kalman Filter, the stochastic characterization of the behavior of dynamical systems, such as the study of fat-tails and fractional brownian motion, the use of data-driven methods (e.g., Kernel Methods, Deep Learning, Dimensionality Reduction), Econophysics methodologies, such as random matrix theory, and the use of geometric and topological tools for the study of critical transitions (e.g., bubble detection) and stochastic resonances.
